## Experiment 1: Rotating Superconductor Disc (1992)

### Setup
- **Material**: YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ (YBCO) high-temperature superconductor
- **Disc**: 145mm diameter × 6mm thick (original); later 275mm diameter
- **Temperature**: 20-70K (liquid nitrogen cooling)
- **Levitation**: Meissner effect, up to 7mm above solenoidal coils
- **Rotation**: 3 or 6 solenoidal coils + 2 toroidal coils, 2-phase mode
- **Speed**: Up to 5,000 RPM
- **Magnetic field frequency**: 50Hz to 10⁶Hz

### Results
- Objects above disc: **0.3-0.5% weight loss**
- When rotation speed slowly reduced: effect increased to **1.9-2.1% weight loss**
- Effect appeared directional — column above disc
- Published in Physica C (peer-reviewed)

### Publication
- Podkletnov, E. & Nieminen, R. "A possibility of gravitational force shielding by bulk YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ superconductor" — Physica C 203 (1992) 441-444
- Follow-up: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0921453402022840

### Replication Attempts
- **Hathaway** (DIRD #10 author): Found NO effect to limits of apparatus sensitivity
- **Li & Torr** (Alabama): Theoretical framework developed, Li's experimental work went classified
- **Fetisov (2022)**: Claims similar results at room temperature with YBCO + crystal hydrate (arXiv: 2209.03332)
- **NASA**: Investigated but no published positive results

### Analysis for This Project
**Podkletnov addressed ONE axis.** The /deeper model predicts full decoupling requires THREE axes. His 0.3-2.1% result is consistent with partial (1/3) decoupling. The parameter sensitivity (Hathaway couldn't replicate) suggests a narrow resonance window.

## Experiment 2: Impulse Gravity Generator (IGG)

### Setup
- **Mechanism**: Marx generator discharge through superconducting emitter in high magnetic field
- **Superconductor**: YBCO composite crystal structure
- **Peak current**: >10,000 amperes (10⁴A)
- **Surface potential**: >1 million volts (later targeting 2MV via Marx generator)
- **Trapped magnetic field**: Up to 1 Tesla
- **Temperature**: Down to 40K
- **Pulse length**: 60-70 nanoseconds

### Results
- **Focused beam** propagating without noticeable attenuation through materials
- Short **repulsive force** on objects along beam axis
- Under certain conditions: **punches holes in brick walls**, deforms inch-thick steel plates
- Light attenuation increased with voltage, up to 7% at 2,000kV

### Propagation Speed
- Measured over 1,211 meters using two piezoelectric sensors + rubidium atomic clocks
- Delay: 631 nanoseconds
- Speed: **~64c** (64 times speed of light)
- Later test: beam over 5 kilometers, penetrated several concrete houses

### Publication
- Podkletnov, E. & Modanese, G. "Investigation of high voltage discharges in low pressure gases through large ceramic superconducting electrodes" — arXiv: physics/0108005

### Replication
- "Null-Results of a Superconducting Gravity-Impulse-Generator" — anomalous radiation energy 9 orders of magnitude smaller
- **Goede Foundation** funding ongoing replication (Marx generator + cryo/vacuum chambers)
